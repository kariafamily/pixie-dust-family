## The Short Answer

Yes. Book it. Ohana Best Friends Breakfast is the best character dining experience at Walt Disney World for families with toddlers, and it is not particularly close.

The characters are Lilo, Stitch, Mickey, and Pluto. The setting is the Great Ceremonial House at Disney's Polynesian Village Resort, overlooking the Seven Seas Lagoon. The food is genuinely good. The character interactions are unhurried and warm. And your toddler will talk about Stitch for the rest of the trip.

We have done it twice. We are already planning to do it again.

## The Characters

Lilo, Stitch, Mickey, and Pluto rotate through the restaurant in a set circuit. Each character stops at every table for a real interaction — not a quick photo and move on, but an actual moment where they engage with your child.

Stitch is the star. He is physical and playful and unpredictable in the best possible way. He stole food off our son's plate, pretended to hide under the table, and spent an unusually long time at our table because our son kept grabbing his antennae. The Cast Member wrangling Stitch played along beautifully.

Lilo is gentle and sweet — she crouched down to our son's level and pointed at his Mickey waffles with genuine delight. The photo we got of the two of them together, waffle skillets in the foreground, is one of our favorite images from all of our Disney trips.

Mickey and Pluto are classics. Our son knew exactly who they were and reacted accordingly. Pluto got down on all fours and our son tried to climb on his back. It was chaos and it was perfect.

## The Food

Family-style service — large skillets come to the table continuously throughout the meal. You are never waiting or flagging down a server for more food.

The Mickey waffles are iconic and genuinely delicious. Scrambled eggs, bacon, sausage, and a breakfast bread pudding that we looked forward to more than we expected. Fresh fruit arrives first. Coffee and juice are included.

The quality is well above average for Disney dining. Everything is hot, everything is replenished quickly, and the portions are generous. We left full every time.

## The Setting

Ohana is on the second floor of the Great Ceremonial House at the Polynesian. The room is open and airy with Polynesian theming throughout — carved wood, tiki elements, and large windows.

Request a lagoon-view table when you make your reservation. On a clear morning you can see Cinderella Castle across the water. We had this view on our second visit and it elevated an already great meal into something genuinely special.

The restaurant fills up quickly and the energy is lively. It is not a quiet breakfast. Toddlers fit right in.

## Booking Tips

Ohana character breakfast books out 60 days in advance. Set a reminder and book the moment your window opens at 6am Eastern time.

We prefer the first seating of the morning — usually around 8am. Characters are fresh, the restaurant is not yet at full chaos energy, and you have the whole day ahead of you after breakfast.

If you are staying at the Polynesian, you can walk downstairs in your pajamas. This is an underrated luxury. If you are staying elsewhere, factor in travel time — the Monorail from Magic Kingdom or the boat from the Transportation and Ticket Center both stop at the Polynesian.

Pricing is around $45-55 per adult and $25-30 per child, subject to change. Check the Disney World dining reservation system for current pricing when you book.

## Grandparent Notes

Ohana breakfast is perfect for multigenerational groups. Leisurely, seated, no walking required. The characters delight grandparents just as much as toddlers — perhaps more, because grandparents appreciate the craftsmanship of the costumes and the dedication of the performers.

My mother-in-law, who had never done character dining before, cried a little when Lilo sat with our son. She has asked to come back specifically for this breakfast on our next trip.

The table size accommodates larger groups comfortably. Make the reservation for your full party.

## Our Honest Bottom Line

Ohana Best Friends Breakfast costs more than a normal breakfast. It takes a reservation made weeks in advance. You need to be at the Polynesian at 8am.

It is worth every bit of that effort. The combination of genuinely good food, unhurried character interactions, a beautiful setting, and the specific magic of watching your toddler meet Stitch for the first time makes this one of the experiences we recommend without hesitation to every family visiting Walt Disney World.

Book it before you book anything else.
