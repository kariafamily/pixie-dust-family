## The Festival Nobody Talks About Enough

Everyone talks about Epcot's Food and Wine Festival in the fall. Fewer people talk about the Flower and Garden Festival, which runs from early March through late May. That is a mistake — especially if you are visiting with toddlers.

The Flower and Garden Festival transforms Epcot into something extraordinary. Elaborate topiaries of beloved Disney characters appear throughout the park. Outdoor kitchens serve seasonal food and drinks in World Showcase. Gardens and floral installations add color and texture to every land.

It is beautiful. It is not crowded the way fall festivals are. And toddlers are absolutely captivated by the giant character topiaries.

## The Topiaries

This is the main event for families with young children. The festival features dozens of Disney character topiaries — sculpted plant figures of characters from across Disney's history, placed throughout the park.

Recent festivals have featured Moana and Maui near the entrance, Winnie the Pooh and friends in the UK pavilion, Mickey and Minnie near Spaceship Earth, and scenes from various Disney films throughout World Showcase. The topiaries change slightly each year.

Our son stood in front of the Moana topiary for a solid five minutes pointing at Pua the pig and saying his name repeatedly. Getting a toddler to pose near a six-foot Maui made of plants is its own kind of magic.

The topiaries are spread throughout the park, which gives you a natural reason to walk through World Showcase — normally a harder sell with very young children. Hunting for the next topiary keeps toddlers engaged through the lagoon walk.

## Outdoor Kitchens

The festival adds outdoor kitchen booths throughout the park serving themed food and drinks. The quality is consistently better than standard park fare and the portions are small-plate sized, which suits a browsing pace.

For families with toddlers, the outdoor kitchens are more parent benefit than toddler benefit. But that matters too. The adults get to eat interesting food and drink seasonal beverages while the toddler is distracted by flowers and topiaries. Everyone wins.

We did a lap of several outdoor kitchens during our son's post-lunch happy window and it was one of the most pleasant hours of any Disney trip we have taken.

## Best Rides at Epcot for Toddlers During the Festival

**Frozen Ever After** in the Norway pavilion — a gentle boat ride through the world of Frozen. Our son was obsessed. Short enough to do twice. Grab a Lightning Lane return time early.

**Remy's Ratatouille Adventure** in the France pavilion — a trackless ride that shrinks you to the size of a rat. Height requirement is 32 inches. Delightful for toddlers who know the movie. Lightning Lane is worth it here.

**Living with the Land** — a slow boat ride through working greenhouses. Genuinely interesting for adults and calming for toddlers. No wait, no Lightning Lane needed.

**Spaceship Earth** — the classic slow journey through human history. Dark but gentle. Good for nap-adjacent moments.

**Gran Fiesta Tour** in the Mexico pavilion — a slow boat ride featuring the Three Caballeros. Completely uncrowded, beautifully themed, zero wait. One of our secret favorites with a toddler.

## Timing Your Visit

The festival runs most of spring — check the exact dates for your year as they shift slightly. Late March through April tends to hit the sweet spot: the topiaries are fully installed and the Florida heat has not yet become punishing.

Arrive at park opening and do Frozen Ever After and Remy immediately before the waits build. Then slow down — the festival rewards a leisurely pace far more than a sprint.

Weekdays are noticeably less crowded than weekends. If your schedule is flexible, a Tuesday or Wednesday visit during the festival is genuinely pleasant.

## Grandparent Notes

The Flower and Garden Festival might be the single best Epcot visit for multigenerational groups. The gardens and floral installations are legitimately beautiful — grandparents appreciate them on a different level than toddlers. World Showcase gives grandparents architecture, culture, and food to engage with while toddlers chase topiary characters.

The pace of a festival visit is naturally slower and more wandering than a standard park day. That suits grandparents well. Less rushing, more stopping to admire things. Our parents have specifically asked to time future trips around this festival.

## Our Honest Take

The Flower and Garden Festival is one of the best-kept secrets at Walt Disney World for families with young children. The topiaries alone are worth timing your trip around. The outdoor kitchens make it a genuine treat for parents. The gardens make it beautiful for grandparents.

If your travel dates overlap with the festival window — roughly early March through late May — plan your Epcot day for during the festival rather than before or after. It is the same park admission price and it is a meaningfully better experience.
