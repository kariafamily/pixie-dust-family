# Disney's Animal Kingdom Lodge Review: Savanna Views, Toddler Magic, Honest Take

**Our Rating: 5/5 stars** &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Toddler Score: 10/10**

Disney's Animal Kingdom Lodge is one of the most distinctive Deluxe resorts at Walt Disney World — a five-story African-inspired lodge wrapped around a private savanna home to giraffes, zebras, wildebeest, ankole cattle, and ostriches. Guests in savanna-view rooms can watch the animals from their own balcony, often within feet of the railing.

We stayed in a savanna-view room with our toddler, and it ended our trip with the highest score we've ever given a Disney resort for a family with a small child. This review is based on a real out-of-pocket stay. Nothing here is sponsored, comped, or paid for by Disney.

![Savanna view from a balcony at Animal Kingdom Lodge with giraffes and zebras](/images/resorts/animal-kingdom-lodge/animal-kingdom-lodge-savanna-wide-view.jpg)

## Quick Take: Is Animal Kingdom Lodge Worth It?

**Yes — and for a toddler family, it's hard to beat.** A savanna-view room turns the resort itself into an attraction. Our son woke up every morning, ran to the balcony, and pointed at giraffes. That alone bought us hours of toddler entertainment without ever leaving the room.

The downsides are real but manageable: the resort is far from the rest of Disney property, the transportation is bus-only, and savanna-view rooms cost a premium over standard. If you're willing to pay for the room view and you build park days around the resort instead of against it, it's the strongest Deluxe at Disney for families with young kids.

## What Are the Room Types at Animal Kingdom Lodge?

Animal Kingdom Lodge is split into two connected buildings:

- **Jambo House** — the original main building, includes hotel rooms and Disney Vacation Club villas
- **Kidani Village** — newer DVC-only building with one-, two-, and three-bedroom villas, plus a separate pool

Room categories from lowest to highest:

1. **Standard View** — parking lot, pool area, or garden
2. **Pool View** — overlooks the Uzima or Samawati Springs pool
3. **Savanna View** — direct view of the resort's wildlife savannas
4. **Club Level (Kilimanjaro Club)** — concierge access, premium lounge with food and drinks throughout the day
5. **Villas (Studios through Grand Villas)** — kitchen or kitchenette, sleeps up to 12 in the largest configurations

> **Pro Tip:** Book a **savanna-view room over a standard or pool view, even if it stretches the budget.** The savanna view is the entire reason to stay here. A standard-view room at Animal Kingdom Lodge is a beautifully themed hotel room — but a savanna-view room is an experience. If you want to bring the cost down without giving up the view, look at **Jambo House DVC studios with savanna view** instead of a standard hotel room. The price-per-night can be similar but you get the kitchenette and slightly larger square footage.

## How Much Does Animal Kingdom Lodge Cost?

Standard rooms typically run **$450-$750 per night** depending on season and view. Savanna view starts around $600-$650. Club Level rooms run $900+. Villas range from studios at $500-$650 to two- and three-bedroom units at $1,500-$3,000+ per night.

Our stay came in around **$4,000 total** including resort dining and one in-room delivery. Animal Kingdom Lodge is one of the better-valued Deluxe options when you factor in the view — comparable savanna access elsewhere costs significantly more or requires a separate animal-experience booking.

> **Pro Tip:** Book early — **6 to 11 months out** — and watch for savanna-view category availability specifically. The savanna-view rooms book up first because they're the headline feature. Disney takes a $200 deposit at booking, but you can **cancel up to 5 days before check-in and get the deposit back in full.** There is almost no downside to locking in dates as soon as you have them. Watch for general public discounts each season and Florida resident rates if those apply.

## Where Is Animal Kingdom Lodge Located and How Do You Get to the Parks?

Animal Kingdom Lodge is located in the far southwest corner of Walt Disney World property — the most remote Deluxe resort. There is no monorail and no boat. Transportation is **buses only.**

| Park / Destination | Method | Approx. Time |
|--------------------|--------|--------------|
| Animal Kingdom | Bus | 5-10 min |
| Magic Kingdom | Bus | 25-35 min |
| EPCOT | Bus | 20-30 min |
| Hollywood Studios | Bus | 15-25 min |
| Disney Springs | Bus | 25-35 min |

The bus to Animal Kingdom is genuinely fast — often the first bus to arrive in the morning queue and the shortest ride. The buses to Magic Kingdom and Disney Springs are the longest of any Deluxe resort.

![Animal Kingdom Lodge entrance sign](/images/resorts/animal-kingdom-lodge/animal-kingdom-lodge-entrance-sign.jpg)

> **Pro Tip:** **Build your trip around Animal Kingdom park on the days you're at this resort.** Bus-only transportation is the resort's biggest knock, but you can flip it into a feature by pairing Animal Kingdom Lodge with Animal Kingdom park days. Hop the 5-minute bus to the park early, ride **Kilimanjaro Safaris first thing,** come back to the resort for lunch and a midday nap on the balcony, then head back to the park for the afternoon. You barely touch a bus more than 6 minutes at a time.

## What's Animal Kingdom Lodge Like for a Toddler?

This is where Animal Kingdom Lodge earns its 10/10. Specifics:

- **The savanna animals.** Visible from the lobby viewing area, multiple balcony views, and dedicated viewing decks. Cast members in green vests are stationed at viewing areas during peak hours to point out animals and answer questions.
- **The lobby.** Massive, soaring six-story space with a real mud fire pit, hand-carved African art, and a glass viewing wall to the savanna. Stroller-friendly. Cool, calm, easy for sensory breaks.
- **Cultural programming.** Free toddler-friendly activities throughout the day — animal viewing tours, African storytelling, art activities, cookie decorating. Check the printed activity schedule at check-in.
- **The pools.** Both pools (Uzima at Jambo House, Samawati Springs at Kidani Village) are zero-entry with waterslides and splash areas.
- **Night Vision.** Free night-vision goggles are loaned out at the savanna viewing area after dark so you can spot animals at night. Toddlers love this.

![Mom and son watching the savanna at sunrise from the balcony](/images/resorts/animal-kingdom-lodge/animal-kingdom-lodge-balcony-mom-son-sunrise.jpg)

> **Pro Tip:** **Request a Sunset Savanna room at Jambo House** when booking. Of the three savannas at the resort, Sunset Savanna gets the most consistent giraffe and zebra activity in the morning and evening — which are also the times a toddler is most likely to be on the balcony with you. Call Disney directly after booking to make the request, or note it during online booking. Requests are not guaranteed but Disney makes a real effort to honor them.

## What Are the Dining Options at Animal Kingdom Lodge?

Animal Kingdom Lodge has some of the strongest dining of any Disney Deluxe.

**Table Service:**
- **Jiko – The Cooking Place** — signature dining, African-inspired, one of the best wine lists at Disney, adult-leaning but kids welcome
- **Boma – Flavors of Africa** — buffet, breakfast and dinner, broad enough variety to satisfy picky toddlers and adventurous adults at the same table
- **Sanaa** (Kidani Village) — Indian-African fusion, table service overlooking a savanna; lunch and dinner

**Quick Service:**
- **The Mara** (Jambo House) — open all day, surprisingly strong menu, easy pickup
- **The Maji Pool Bar** — drinks and snacks poolside

**Lounges:**
- **Victoria Falls Lounge** — sits between Jiko and Boma, African wine and cocktails, perfect for a quiet drink while one parent does bedtime

> **Pro Tip:** **Book Boma for dinner with a toddler — not Jiko.** Boma is a buffet, which means there's something every picky toddler will eat (carved meats, mac and cheese, pasta, fruit), you're not stuck waiting on a slow kitchen, and you can get up and reset if your kid melts down. Jiko is gorgeous but slow-paced and adult-leaning. Save Jiko for a date night when grandparents are watching the toddler back in the room.

## What's the Pool Situation?

Two pools, both excellent:

1. **Uzima Springs (Jambo House)** — main pool, zero-entry, themed African rock formation, 67-foot waterslide, separate kiddie pool, two hot tubs. Pool bar adjacent.
2. **Samawati Springs (Kidani Village)** — smaller, themed around an African watering hole, zero-entry, 128-foot waterslide, larger kid play area with shallow splash zones.

Both are heated year-round. Towels provided. Guests at either building can use both pools.

> **Pro Tip:** **Use Samawati Springs at Kidani Village if you have a younger toddler.** The kid play area there is bigger, shallower, and less crowded than the main pool at Jambo House. A short walking path connects the two buildings.

## What About Grandparents and Multigenerational Travel?

Animal Kingdom Lodge is an unusually strong choice for multigenerational trips, even though we didn't have grandparents on this specific stay. Quick planning notes:

- **The savanna view works for every age.** Grandparents who want to opt out of a park day can spend hours on a balcony or in the lobby savanna viewing area without ever feeling like they missed out.
- **Two- and three-bedroom DVC villas at Kidani Village** are some of the largest accommodations at Disney — built for multigenerational stays with separate bedrooms, full kitchen, washer/dryer, and balcony.
- **Boma's buffet** is the easiest dinner solution for a group with different eating preferences across three generations.
- **Cultural programming and animal viewing tours** are sit-and-watch friendly — no walking required.
- **Bus-only transportation** is the one caveat. Anyone with mobility issues should request a room near the elevators and the bus stop. Disney's wheelchair-accessible rooms are available — note the need during booking.

## What Did We Not Love?

Honest negatives:

- **The remoteness.** The bus ride to Magic Kingdom, EPCOT, or Disney Springs is long. If your trip is heavy on Magic Kingdom, you'll feel it.
- **Bus-only transportation.** No monorail, no boat, no Skyliner. If a bus is late or full, you wait.
- **Savanna view pricing.** Paying $150-$250 more per night for the view is real money. It's worth it once — but for a longer trip we'd consider mixing in nights at a different resort.
- **Long internal walking distances.** From the farthest Jambo House rooms to the lobby and bus stop is a real walk. Request a room closer to the main building if mobility is a concern.

We'd come back without hesitation — this is one of the resorts we actively plan return trips around.

## Animal Kingdom Lodge vs. Wilderness Lodge — Which Themed Deluxe Is Better for Toddlers?

Both are nature-themed Deluxe resorts with strong theming. Short version:

- **Animal Kingdom Lodge** — savanna animals from the room, better dining, larger lobby spaces, but farther from the rest of Disney and bus-only
- **Wilderness Lodge** — closer to Magic Kingdom (boat access), forest-and-geyser theming, smaller scale, but no in-room wildlife

For a toddler family who wants the resort itself to be an attraction, Animal Kingdom Lodge wins. For a family who prioritizes Magic Kingdom access, Wilderness Lodge edges ahead.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can you see animals from your room at Animal Kingdom Lodge?**
Only if you book a **savanna-view room.** Standard-view and pool-view rooms do not have animal views from the balcony.

**Is Animal Kingdom Lodge on the monorail?**
No. Animal Kingdom Lodge is bus-only for park transportation.

**How far is Animal Kingdom Lodge from Magic Kingdom?**
The bus ride is approximately 25-35 minutes depending on time of day and traffic.

**Is Animal Kingdom Lodge good for toddlers?**
Yes — it's one of the most toddler-friendly Deluxe resorts at Disney. The savanna view, free cultural programming, zero-entry pools, and on-site dining make it exceptional for families with young children.

**How much does a savanna-view room cost?**
Savanna-view rooms typically run $600-$900 per night depending on season and specific savanna location.

**Does Animal Kingdom Lodge have free parking?**
Yes, self-parking is complimentary for all resort guests. Valet is available for an additional fee.

**What animals can you see at Animal Kingdom Lodge?**
The resort hosts giraffes, zebras, wildebeest, ostriches, ankole cattle, kudu, gerenuk, impala, eland, bongo, and more across three savannas (Arusha, Sunset, and Pembe).

**Is Boma worth it for picky eaters?**
Yes. Despite the African-inspired theme, the Boma buffet includes many simple, kid-friendly options (carved meats, mac and cheese, pasta, fruit, breads, desserts).

**What's the difference between Jambo House and Kidani Village?**
Jambo House is the original building with hotel rooms, DVC studios, and the main lobby. Kidani Village is a separate DVC-only building (no hotel rooms) with its own pool and dining (Sanaa).

**Can you do a safari tour at Animal Kingdom Lodge?**
Yes — paid options include the **Sunrise Safari** and **Wanyama Safari & Dining Experience** (adults only). Free animal viewing happens 24/7 from public viewing areas and savanna-view rooms.

**Does Animal Kingdom Lodge have a kids' club?**
No. The former kids' club, Simba's Cubhouse, closed and has not reopened.

**Are pets allowed at Animal Kingdom Lodge?**
No. Animal Kingdom Lodge is not one of Disney's pet-friendly resorts.

**Do you need a car at Animal Kingdom Lodge?**
No, Disney transportation reaches every park and Disney Springs by bus. A car can be useful if you plan to dine off-property but is not required.

**Is the savanna view worth the extra cost?**
For most guests staying 3+ nights with a strong interest in animals, yes — the view is the headline feature of the resort. For a one-night stay, a standard or pool view may be more cost-efficient.

**What's the best time to visit Animal Kingdom Lodge with a toddler?**
Early November and mid-January through mid-February tend to offer lower crowds and mild Florida weather. The animals are also most visible in cooler months when they spend more time grazing in open areas.
