# Disney's Beach Club Resort Review: Honest Take from a Toddler Family

**Our Rating: 4/5 stars** &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Toddler Score: 9.2/10**

Disney's Beach Club Resort is a New England seaside-themed Deluxe resort sitting on Crescent Lake between EPCOT and Disney's Hollywood Studios. It shares **Stormalong Bay** — widely considered the best pool at any Disney resort — with the neighboring Yacht Club, and offers walking access to EPCOT's International Gateway entrance.

We stayed here with our toddler. This review is based on a real out-of-pocket stay. Nothing here is sponsored, comped, or paid for by Disney.

## Quick Take: Is the Beach Club Worth It?

**Yes — and the pool alone is enough to justify it.** Stormalong Bay is a 3-acre sand-bottom water complex with a slow river, multiple shallow zones for toddlers, a sand play area, and a pirate-ship waterslide. If your family will swim more than a few hours each day, this is the resort that pays back hardest.

The walking path to EPCOT is the other big win — back of the park entrance, no transportation needed. Hollywood Studios is a 20-minute walk or a Skyliner ride away. For an EPCOT-and-Hollywood-Studios-heavy trip, this is the easiest Deluxe at Disney.

The downsides: getting to Magic Kingdom is slow (no monorail, no boat), standard rooms are mid-sized, and the resort theming — while charming — doesn't carry the same "wow" factor as the Polynesian, Grand Floridian, or Animal Kingdom Lodge.

## What Are the Room Types at the Beach Club?

The Beach Club has two main components:

- **Beach Club Resort (hotel rooms)** — standard hotel rooms in the main building
- **Beach Club Villas** — separate DVC building with studios, one-bedrooms, and two-bedrooms

Room categories from lowest to highest:

1. **Standard View** — parking lot or rooftop
2. **Garden/Woods View** — landscaped grounds
3. **Water/Pool View** — Stormalong Bay or quiet pool
4. **Lagoon View** — Crescent Lake
5. **Club Level / Stone Harbor Club** — concierge access on the fifth floor
6. **Villas (DVC)** — kitchenette or full kitchen, larger units

> **Pro Tip:** If you want the best pool experience without the Pool View premium, **request a low-floor room facing Stormalong Bay.** A low-floor pool-adjacent room makes it easy to run the toddler back for a nap, a diaper change, or a snack without trekking through the lobby. Bonus: you'll hear the pool but the noise is minimal after lifeguards leave for the day around 10 PM.

## How Much Does the Beach Club Cost?

Standard rooms typically run **$600-$900 per night** depending on season and view. Club Level rooms run $1,100+. DVC villa rates vary widely; studios run $550-$750 and two-bedrooms are $1,500+.

Beach Club is mid-range among Deluxe resorts — more expensive than Animal Kingdom Lodge and Wilderness Lodge but generally cheaper than the Polynesian or Grand Floridian.

> **Pro Tip:** Book early — **6 to 11 months out** — for best rate and category availability. Disney takes a $200 deposit but you can **cancel up to 5 days before check-in and get the deposit back in full**, so locking in dates early carries almost no risk. Beach Club is a frequent target for general public discounts; check for promotions before paying rack rate.

## Where Is the Beach Club Located and How Do You Get to the Parks?

The Beach Club is part of the **EPCOT Resort Area** along with Yacht Club, BoardWalk Inn, Swan, and Dolphin. Transportation:

| Park / Destination | Method | Approx. Time |
|--------------------|--------|--------------|
| EPCOT (International Gateway) | Walking path | 5-10 min |
| Hollywood Studios | Walking path | 15-20 min |
| Hollywood Studios | Skyliner | 10-15 min |
| Hollywood Studios | Boat | 15-20 min |
| Magic Kingdom | Bus | 25-30 min |
| Animal Kingdom | Bus | 25-30 min |
| Disney Springs | Bus | 25-30 min |
| BoardWalk | Walking path | 5-10 min |

The walking path to EPCOT drops you at the **International Gateway** entrance — behind France in the World Showcase. This is genuinely one of the best Disney transportation perks. Stroller-accessible, flat, scenic.

> **Pro Tip:** **For Hollywood Studios, take the Skyliner over the walking path or the boat.** The Skyliner station is a short walk from the Beach Club via the BoardWalk. It's faster than walking, faster than the boat, and toddlers love riding in the gondolas. Plus, you skip the bus crowd. The Skyliner stops running in severe weather, so check the weather forecast before counting on it as your only transportation.

## What's the Beach Club Like for a Toddler?

Toddler-friendly in real, structural ways:

- **Stormalong Bay.** The 3-acre sand-bottom pool complex has zero-entry shallow areas, a kiddie pool with a small slide, a slow river, and a pirate-ship waterslide. The sand bottom (real sand, not concrete) is gentler on toddler knees.
- **Beaches & Cream Soda Shop.** On-site ice cream parlor with classic shop atmosphere, kids' menu, and one of the most photographed sundaes at Disney (The Kitchen Sink).
- **The walking path to EPCOT.** A toddler can be back in their pajamas in their hotel room 10 minutes after leaving the park.
- **The BoardWalk.** A 5-minute walk to the BoardWalk's mini-golf, midway games, and street performers in the evening.
- **Beach with sand.** A small sand beach faces Crescent Lake with chairs and a swing — not for swimming, but great for play and sunset views.

> **Pro Tip:** **Hit Stormalong Bay first thing in the morning before park time.** Pool opens early (typically 10 AM), and from 10-11:30 AM it's still relatively empty. Toddlers tire out, you can be in the parks by lunch, and the pool stays the highlight of the trip. The sand on the pool bottom is a real upgrade over concrete — bring water shoes anyway for the splash zones.

## What Are the Dining Options at the Beach Club?

Solid lineup with some Disney signatures:

**Table Service:**
- **Cape May Cafe** — character breakfast (Goofy, Donald, Minnie), seafood buffet dinner
- **Beaches & Cream Soda Shop** — table service ice cream parlor, burgers, sandwiches, the famous Kitchen Sink sundae
- **Ale & Compass Restaurant** (Yacht Club, walkable) — New England seafood and steaks

**Quick Service:**
- **Beach Club Marketplace** — open all day, grab-and-go, sandwiches, pastries
- **Hurricane Hanna's Waterside Bar & Grill** — poolside

**Lounges:**
- **Martha's Vineyard** — wine-focused lounge between the lobby and Beaches & Cream
- **Ale & Compass Lounge** (Yacht Club) — bar with full menu

**Walkable to:**
- **All of the BoardWalk dining** including Trattoria al Forno, Big River Grille, and ESPN Club
- **Disney's Yacht Club** dining
- **Swan and Dolphin** signature restaurants (Il Mulino, Shula's, Bluezoo)

> **Pro Tip:** **Book Cape May Cafe breakfast over 'Ohana if you want character interaction without crossing Disney property.** It's right downstairs from your room, the characters (Goofy, Donald, Minnie) are toddler-friendly, and you can be at EPCOT 30 minutes after breakfast. Reservations open exactly **60 days before check-in at 6 AM Eastern.**

## What's the Pool Situation?

**Stormalong Bay** is the headline attraction:

1. **Stormalong Bay** — shared with the Yacht Club; 3 acres of sand-bottom water; multiple zones (deep swim area, slow river, kiddie pool, pirate-ship waterslide); sand play area on the side; pool bar; two hot tubs
2. **Beach Club Villas Quiet Pool** — smaller, calmer pool near the DVC building
3. **Beach Club Quiet Pool** — small pool near the Beach Club itself

Stormalong Bay requires a Beach Club, Yacht Club, or Beach Club Villas room key to enter (this rule is enforced). Lifeguards on duty. Towels provided.

> **Pro Tip:** **The pirate-ship waterslide has a minimum height requirement (60 inches)**, which most toddlers won't meet. That's fine — there is plenty of toddler-appropriate water elsewhere in the complex. Just set expectations with older kids and toddlers separately so the older one doesn't think the slide will be available the entire trip.

## What About Grandparents and Multigenerational Travel?

Strong choice for multigenerational trips, with a few specific considerations:

- **Walking access to two parks** (EPCOT and Hollywood Studios) is gentler than bus transportation for grandparents with mobility considerations.
- **Stormalong Bay's slow river and shallow zones** work for grandparents who want to swim with grandkids without doing serious swimming.
- **Two-bedroom Beach Club Villas** provide separate bedroom and full kitchen for a multigenerational family.
- **Cape May Cafe breakfast** is a low-effort shared character experience that works across generations.
- **Beach Club lobby and walking paths** are flat and stroller/wheelchair friendly.

## What Did We Not Love?

Honest negatives:

- **Magic Kingdom transportation.** Bus-only, 25-30 minutes. If Magic Kingdom is your primary park, the Beach Club is the wrong base.
- **Room size.** Standard rooms are smaller than at the Polynesian or Grand Floridian. For a family of three with a Pack 'n Play, it's manageable but tight.
- **Theming is subtle.** The New England seaside theme is well-executed but doesn't have the immediate "you're at Disney" wow of the Polynesian or Animal Kingdom Lodge.
- **Stormalong Bay can be crowded.** During peak season afternoons, finding a chair is genuinely hard. Get to the pool early or go in the morning.

We'd come back, especially for an EPCOT-heavy trip — but for a Magic Kingdom-focused vacation we'd stay elsewhere.

## Beach Club vs. BoardWalk Inn — Which EPCOT Area Resort Is Best for Toddlers?

Both are within walking distance of EPCOT. Key differences:

- **Beach Club** — best pool at Disney (Stormalong Bay), quieter atmosphere, slightly closer to EPCOT entrance
- **BoardWalk Inn** — more entertainment options on the boardwalk itself, smaller pool, more nightlife (which can be a plus or a minus with a toddler)

For a toddler family who'll spend serious time at the pool, the Beach Club wins. For a family who wants on-property entertainment options every night, the BoardWalk Inn edges ahead.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is the Beach Club on the monorail?**
No. The Beach Club uses walking paths, buses, the Skyliner, and Friendship boats for transportation.

**Can you walk to EPCOT from the Beach Club?**
Yes. A paved, stroller-accessible walking path runs from the Beach Club to EPCOT's International Gateway entrance. The walk takes 5-10 minutes.

**Can you walk to Hollywood Studios from the Beach Club?**
Yes, the walk takes 15-20 minutes. The Skyliner and Friendship boats are faster alternatives.

**Is Stormalong Bay open to all guests?**
No. Stormalong Bay is exclusive to guests of the Beach Club, Yacht Club, and Beach Club Villas. Access is enforced by room key.

**Does Stormalong Bay have a real sand bottom?**
Yes. The bottom of the pool is filled with actual sand, which is gentler on knees and feet than concrete. The sand is filtered and maintained as part of the pool system.

**How much does the Beach Club cost per night?**
Standard rooms run roughly $600-$900 per night. Club Level rooms run higher. DVC villa rates vary by category and season.

**Does the Beach Club have free parking?**
Yes, self-parking is complimentary for all resort guests. Valet is available for an additional fee.

**Is the Beach Club good for toddlers?**
Yes. Stormalong Bay's shallow zones and sand bottom, walking access to EPCOT, and Beaches & Cream Soda Shop make it one of the strongest Deluxe resorts for families with young children.

**Can you see fireworks from the Beach Club?**
You can see EPCOT's nighttime spectacular from lagoon-view rooms and the BoardWalk. Magic Kingdom fireworks are not visible from the Beach Club.

**Are pets allowed at the Beach Club?**
No. The Beach Club is not one of Disney's pet-friendly resorts.

**Do you need a car at the Beach Club?**
No. Disney transportation reaches every park, Disney Springs, and the water parks via walking paths, buses, boats, and the Skyliner. A car can be useful for off-property dining but is not required.

**Is the Skyliner included in the Disney transportation system?**
Yes. The Skyliner is free for resort guests and runs between the Caribbean Beach, Riviera, Pop Century, Art of Animation, Beach Club, BoardWalk Inn, and Hollywood Studios via a transfer station.

**Is Beaches & Cream worth the reservation?**
For families with young kids, yes. The Kitchen Sink sundae is a classic experience and the casual atmosphere works well for toddlers. Reservations book up early — book 60 days out.

**What's the difference between the Beach Club and the Yacht Club?**
They are sister resorts sharing Stormalong Bay. The Beach Club has a casual New England seaside theme; the Yacht Club has a more formal nautical theme. Yacht Club rooms tend to be slightly larger.

**What's the best time to visit the Beach Club with a toddler?**
Early November and mid-January through mid-February typically offer lower crowds and milder Florida weather — both ideal for toddler-paced touring and outdoor pool time.
