# Disney's BoardWalk Inn Review: Honest Take from a Toddler Family

**Our Rating: 3/5 stars** &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Toddler Score: 7.5/10**

Disney's BoardWalk Inn is a Deluxe resort designed around an early-1900s Atlantic City boardwalk theme. It sits on Crescent Lake directly across from the Yacht Club and Beach Club, with the resort's namesake boardwalk running along the water — lined with restaurants, lounges, midway games, and street performers in the evenings.

We stayed here with our toddler. This review is based on a real out-of-pocket stay. Nothing here is sponsored, comped, or paid for by Disney.

## Quick Take: Is the BoardWalk Inn Worth It?

**Probably not — at least not over the Beach Club next door.** The BoardWalk Inn shares the same walking access to EPCOT and Hollywood Studios as the Beach Club, but the pool is much weaker (no Stormalong Bay access), the rooms run on the older side compared to recent Disney refurbs, and the dining lineup, while good, doesn't pull ahead of nearby options.

Where the BoardWalk Inn does win: the on-property nightlife. The boardwalk itself — with its midway games, street performers, ice cream stand, dueling pianos at Jellyrolls — is a genuinely fun atmosphere in the evening. If your family loves the boardwalk vibe and you want to walk out your front door into it every night, this is the resort for that. For a toddler family that goes to bed at 7 PM, the boardwalk doesn't add as much value.

## What Are the Room Types at the BoardWalk Inn?

The BoardWalk has two main components:

- **BoardWalk Inn (hotel rooms)** — standard hotel rooms in the main building
- **BoardWalk Villas** — separate DVC building with studios, one-bedrooms, two-bedrooms, and Grand Villas

Room categories from lowest to highest:

1. **Standard View** — parking lot, rooftop, or limited view
2. **Garden/Pool View** — landscaped courtyard or quiet pool
3. **Water View** — Crescent Lake
4. **BoardWalk View** — directly overlooks the boardwalk and lake (note: can be loud at night)
5. **Club Level / Innkeeper's Club** — concierge access
6. **Garden Suites** — themed standalone-style suites with private entrances
7. **Villas (DVC)** — kitchenette or full kitchen

> **Pro Tip:** With a toddler, **avoid BoardWalk View rooms.** They look spectacular, but the boardwalk itself runs entertainment until 11 PM or later — dueling pianos, street performers, crowds. Beautiful for adults, brutal for toddler bedtimes. Request a **Garden View or Pool View** instead. You'll still hear the energy faintly, but it won't be in your room.

## How Much Does the BoardWalk Inn Cost?

Standard rooms typically run **$550-$850 per night** depending on season and view. Club Level rooms run $1,100+. Garden Suites run $1,300+. DVC villa rates vary widely; Grand Villas can be $3,000+.

BoardWalk Inn is mid-range among Deluxe resorts — comparable pricing to Beach Club, slightly cheaper than Polynesian or Grand Floridian.

> **Pro Tip:** **Run the Beach Club vs BoardWalk Inn price comparison side by side for your specific dates before booking.** For most dates they price within $50/night of each other. The Beach Club's pool advantage is significant, so unless the BoardWalk comes in meaningfully cheaper or has the only available room, the Beach Club is usually the better deal. Disney's $200 deposit is **fully refundable up to 5 days before check-in**, so book one and switch if a better rate or category opens up.

## Where Is the BoardWalk Inn Located and How Do You Get to the Parks?

The BoardWalk Inn is part of the **EPCOT Resort Area** along with Beach Club, Yacht Club, Swan, and Dolphin. Transportation:

| Park / Destination | Method | Approx. Time |
|--------------------|--------|--------------|
| EPCOT (International Gateway) | Walking path | 5-10 min |
| Hollywood Studios | Walking path | 15-20 min |
| Hollywood Studios | Skyliner | 10-15 min |
| Hollywood Studios | Boat | 15-20 min |
| Magic Kingdom | Bus | 25-30 min |
| Animal Kingdom | Bus | 25-30 min |
| Disney Springs | Bus | 25-30 min |
| Beach Club / Yacht Club | Walking path | 5 min |

Walking access to EPCOT's International Gateway is the headline transportation perk. Stroller-accessible, flat, scenic. Skyliner station is on the boardwalk near the Yacht/Beach Club side.

> **Pro Tip:** **The walk from the BoardWalk to Hollywood Studios is doable but long with a toddler.** Use the Skyliner or Friendship boats for Hollywood Studios trips, not the walking path. Save your toddler's energy for the parks themselves. The Skyliner stops in severe weather, so always check the weather and have a bus backup plan.

## What's the BoardWalk Inn Like for a Toddler?

Mixed — strong in some ways, weaker in others:

- **The boardwalk itself.** Carnival-style games, performers, ice cream stand (BoardWalk Ice Cream), classic atmosphere. Toddlers love it in small doses.
- **Surrey bike rentals.** Family-sized pedal carts available for rent on the boardwalk. Older toddlers love them. Younger toddlers ride in the front seat.
- **Walking access to EPCOT.** Easy in-and-out, back to the room for naps.
- **The Skyliner.** Toddlers love riding in gondolas. A 5-minute walk to the station.
- **Quiet courtyards.** The hotel itself has quieter interior courtyards away from the boardwalk noise.

What it lacks for toddlers:

- **A great pool.** Luna Park Pool is fine but nothing like Stormalong Bay. The "Keister Coaster" waterslide is themed nicely but the pool overall is smaller and less feature-rich than the EPCOT-area alternatives.
- **A toddler-friendly character meal.** No on-site character dining at the BoardWalk.
- **Beach access.** No real beach (Beach Club has one, BoardWalk Inn does not).

> **Pro Tip:** **Take advantage of being neighbors with the Beach Club.** Many of the toddler-friendly features that the BoardWalk Inn doesn't have are a 5-minute walk away. Cape May Cafe for character breakfast, the Beach Club's small sand beach, Beaches & Cream Soda Shop — all walkable. You can't use Stormalong Bay (room key enforced), but you can dine, ride the Skyliner, and walk the area freely.

## What Are the Dining Options at the BoardWalk Inn?

Strong dining lineup — possibly the best part of staying here.

**Table Service (on the boardwalk):**
- **Trattoria al Forno** — table service Italian, breakfast and dinner; one of the best Italian options at Disney
- **Flying Fish** — signature dining, contemporary seafood, smart casual
- **Big River Grille & Brewing Works** — casual American with on-site microbrews; one of the few places at Disney to get actual craft beer brewed on property
- **ESPN Club** — sports bar with TVs everywhere, casual menu

**Quick Service (on the boardwalk):**
- **BoardWalk Bakery** — open all day, pastries, sandwiches, kids' meals
- **Pizza Window** — late-night pizza by the slice
- **BoardWalk Joe's Marvelous Margaritas** — frozen drinks
- **BoardWalk Ice Cream** — soft-serve and hand-scooped

**Lounges:**
- **Jellyrolls** — dueling pianos, 21+ after 7 PM
- **Atlantic Dance Hall** — dance club, 21+ (operating hours vary)
- **Belle Vue Lounge** — quiet lounge near the lobby

**Walkable to:**
- **All Beach Club and Yacht Club dining**
- **Swan and Dolphin dining** (Il Mulino, Shula's, Bluezoo)

> **Pro Tip:** **Book Trattoria al Forno breakfast.** It's open early (typically 7:30 AM), pasta-and-meatballs-for-breakfast adjacent menu items work for picky toddlers, the kitchen is fast, and it's a 30-second walk from BoardWalk Inn rooms. Reservations open exactly **60 days before check-in at 6 AM Eastern.** Strong breakfast option without crossing Disney property.

## What's the Pool Situation?

**One main pool, plus quiet pools:**

1. **Luna Park Pool** — themed around a Coney Island-style amusement park; features the "Keister Coaster" clown-faced waterslide; zero-entry section, hot tub, kiddie play area, pool bar
2. **Quiet pools** — two smaller pools scattered through the resort and the Villas

The pool is the BoardWalk Inn's biggest weakness compared to neighboring Beach Club. It's a perfectly fine resort pool — just not a destination pool. Towels provided. Lifeguards on duty.

> **Pro Tip:** **If pool time is a major part of your trip, this is the wrong resort.** Even if the BoardWalk price beats the Beach Club, the pool gap is real. Either book the Beach Club for Stormalong Bay access, or stay at the BoardWalk and plan to spend less pool time and more park/dining time.

## What About Grandparents and Multigenerational Travel?

Decent fit for multigenerational trips, with some specific notes:

- **The boardwalk atmosphere** is a strong evening shared experience for grandparents who enjoy strolling and people-watching.
- **Walking access to EPCOT** is gentler than bus transportation for guests with mobility considerations. Stroller and wheelchair friendly.
- **Grand Villas (DVC)** are some of the largest accommodations at Disney — three bedrooms, full kitchen, two bathrooms, designed for multigenerational stays.
- **Trattoria al Forno** is a strong shared dining experience that works across generations.
- **Carriage rides** along the boardwalk in the evening can be a memorable multigenerational moment.

## What Did We Not Love?

Honest negatives:

- **The price-to-value ratio.** At Beach Club prices, you can get Stormalong Bay. The BoardWalk Inn's pool isn't a fair trade.
- **Standard rooms feel older.** The hotel rooms haven't been refurbished as recently as some other Deluxes. Wear shows in places.
- **BoardWalk View rooms can be loud.** If you booked one, it's beautiful — but with a toddler bedtime, the energy outside can spill in.
- **No character dining on-site.** A real shortfall for families specifically looking for character meals.
- **The interior layout can be confusing.** The hotel building wraps around in a way that makes finding your room from the lobby take a couple of trips to figure out.

We probably wouldn't come back unless the price was meaningfully better than Beach Club for our specific dates — or unless we were on an adults-mostly trip where the boardwalk atmosphere itself was the draw.

## BoardWalk Inn vs. Beach Club — Which EPCOT Resort Is Best for Toddlers?

Both are in the EPCOT Resort Area within walking distance of EPCOT. Key differences:

- **Beach Club** — best pool at Disney (Stormalong Bay), small sand beach, on-site character breakfast at Cape May Cafe, slightly closer to EPCOT entrance
- **BoardWalk Inn** — boardwalk atmosphere with games and entertainment, more variety of nearby dining, microbrews and dueling pianos, smaller pool, no character dining

For a toddler family, the Beach Club wins on every metric that matters most for young kids: pool, character dining, beach.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is the BoardWalk Inn on the monorail?**
No. The BoardWalk Inn uses walking paths, buses, the Skyliner, and Friendship boats for transportation.

**Can you walk to EPCOT from the BoardWalk Inn?**
Yes. A paved, stroller-accessible walking path runs from the BoardWalk Inn to EPCOT's International Gateway entrance. The walk takes 5-10 minutes.

**Can you walk to Hollywood Studios from the BoardWalk Inn?**
Yes, the walk takes 15-20 minutes. The Skyliner and Friendship boats are faster.

**Is the BoardWalk Inn good for toddlers?**
It's adequate but not exceptional. Walking access to EPCOT is a strong toddler perk. The pool is below average compared to the neighboring Beach Club, and there is no on-site character dining.

**How much does the BoardWalk Inn cost per night?**
Standard rooms run roughly $550-$850 per night. Club Level and Garden Suites run higher. DVC villa rates vary widely by category.

**Does the BoardWalk Inn have free parking?**
Yes, self-parking is complimentary for all resort guests. Valet is available for an additional fee.

**Can guests at the BoardWalk Inn use Stormalong Bay?**
No. Stormalong Bay is exclusive to guests of Beach Club, Yacht Club, and Beach Club Villas. Access is enforced by room key.

**Is the BoardWalk Inn noisy at night?**
BoardWalk View rooms can be noisy until 11 PM or later due to entertainment along the boardwalk. Garden View and Pool View rooms are significantly quieter.

**What's on the BoardWalk itself?**
The boardwalk features midway-style games, street performers (jugglers, magicians, musicians), surrey bike rentals, ice cream stands, restaurants, dueling piano bar (Jellyrolls), and a dance hall (Atlantic Dance Hall).

**Can you see fireworks from the BoardWalk Inn?**
Yes, EPCOT's nighttime spectacular is visible from BoardWalk View rooms and from many points along the boardwalk itself.

**Are pets allowed at the BoardWalk Inn?**
No. The BoardWalk Inn is not one of Disney's pet-friendly resorts.

**Do you need a car at the BoardWalk Inn?**
No. Disney transportation reaches every park, Disney Springs, and the water parks. A car can be useful for off-property dining but is not required.

**Is the BoardWalk Inn or Beach Club better for families?**
For most families with young children, the Beach Club is the stronger choice due to Stormalong Bay and on-site character dining. The BoardWalk Inn is a better fit for families that prioritize boardwalk entertainment.

**What's the best time to visit the BoardWalk Inn with a toddler?**
Early November and mid-January through mid-February typically offer lower crowds and milder Florida weather.

**What is Jellyrolls?**
Jellyrolls is a dueling pianos bar on the boardwalk. It is 21+ after 7 PM, so it is not a family destination. Note the proximity if you book a BoardWalk View room.
