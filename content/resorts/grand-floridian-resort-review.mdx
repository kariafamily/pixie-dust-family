# Disney's Grand Floridian Resort & Spa Review: Honest Take from a Toddler Family

**Our Rating: 4/5 stars** &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Toddler Score: 8/10**

Disney's Grand Floridian Resort & Spa is Walt Disney World's flagship Deluxe resort — a Victorian-style hotel on the Seven Seas Lagoon with monorail access, a walking path directly to Magic Kingdom, and the most formal atmosphere of any Disney resort. It's frequently the choice for honeymoons, anniversaries, and multigenerational celebrations.

We stayed here with our toddler. This review is based on a real out-of-pocket stay. Nothing here is sponsored, comped, or paid for by Disney.

## Quick Take: Is the Grand Floridian Worth It?

**For most toddler families, yes — but with a caveat.** The walking path to Magic Kingdom is one of the most underrated perks at Disney World. You can leave Magic Kingdom mid-meltdown and be in your hotel room in 15 minutes without ever waiting for transportation. The monorail is right there too. The lobby is gorgeous, the grounds are pristine, and the dining is top-tier.

The caveat: the Grand Floridian is the most formal resort at Disney. The atmosphere is built around adults — afternoon tea, live piano in the lobby, fine dining. Toddler chaos is welcomed (this is still Disney), but the vibe is not as effortlessly kid-energetic as the Polynesian. If you want unbridled family fun, the Polynesian wins. If you want refined Disney with toddler-friendly access to Magic Kingdom, this is it.

## What Are the Room Types at the Grand Floridian?

The Grand Floridian has a main building and several outer "lodge" buildings, plus DVC villas:

- **Main Building** — concierge rooms only, on the upper floors above the lobby
- **Outer Lodge Buildings** (Sago Cay, Conch Key, Boca Chica, Big Pine Key, Sugar Loaf) — standard hotel rooms
- **Villas at Disney's Grand Floridian Resort & Spa** — DVC studios, one-bedrooms, two-bedrooms, and three-bedroom Grand Villas

Room categories from lowest to highest:

1. **Standard View** — landscaped grounds or parking lot
2. **Garden View** — landscaped courtyard or pool
3. **Lagoon View** — Seven Seas Lagoon, partial castle views
4. **Theme Park View** — direct line to Cinderella Castle
5. **Club Level / Royal Palm Club** — concierge access in the main building
6. **Villas** — kitchenette or full kitchen, larger square footage

> **Pro Tip:** If you're booking a standard room, **request Sugar Loaf Key.** It's the closest outer building to the main lobby, the monorail, and the walking path to Magic Kingdom. The other outer buildings can be a 7-10 minute walk from the lobby — a real penalty when you have a tired toddler. Sugar Loaf also has its own small concierge lounge for some room categories.

## How Much Does the Grand Floridian Cost?

Standard rooms typically run **$750-$1,200 per night** depending on season and view. Theme Park View pushes past $1,300. Club Level and Royal Palm Club rooms run $1,500+. Grand Villas can run $4,000+.

The Grand Floridian is consistently the **most expensive Deluxe resort at Disney.** You're paying for the address, the architecture, and the proximity to Magic Kingdom.

> **Pro Tip:** Book early — **6 to 11 months out** — and watch for general public discounts, which the Grand Floridian receives more often than newcomers expect. Disney takes a $200 deposit, but you can **cancel up to 5 days before check-in and get the deposit back in full**, so locking in early carries almost no risk. If you have Florida resident status, watch for Florida resident-exclusive rates — they can knock $150+ per night off the Grand.

## Where Is the Grand Floridian Located and How Do You Get to the Parks?

The Grand Floridian sits on the Seven Seas Lagoon, between the Polynesian and Magic Kingdom. Transportation:

| Park / Destination | Method | Approx. Time |
|--------------------|--------|--------------|
| Magic Kingdom | Walking path | 10-15 min |
| Magic Kingdom | Monorail | 5-10 min |
| Magic Kingdom | Boat | 10 min |
| EPCOT | Monorail (transfer at TTC) | 20-25 min |
| Hollywood Studios | Bus | 20-25 min |
| Animal Kingdom | Bus | 25-30 min |
| Disney Springs | Bus | 25-30 min |

The **walking path to Magic Kingdom** is the underrated transportation perk. It's a flat, paved, fully stroller-accessible path that runs along the lagoon. About 10-15 minutes one way. When the monorail is down or the boat line is long, the walking path is unaffected.

> **Pro Tip:** **Use the walking path going INTO Magic Kingdom in the morning, and the monorail going BACK in the afternoon.** Mornings are when you have the most toddler energy and the walking path is shaded and pleasant. Afternoons are when the toddler is melting down — load them on the monorail, sit down, and be back at the room in 10 minutes. The path is also useful at the end of fireworks when the monorail is jammed.

## What's the Grand Floridian Like for a Toddler?

Good, but the resort is built around adults more than kids. Specifics:

- **The lobby.** Five-story Victorian atrium with a working pipe organ, live piano music throughout the day, and a band that plays in the evenings. Open seating, stroller-friendly, but quieter than other Disney lobbies.
- **The pool area.** Beach Pool has a zero-entry section and a 181-foot waterslide themed as an old water tower. There's a separate Courtyard Pool that's quieter.
- **The beach.** A small white-sand beach on the Seven Seas Lagoon, with views of Magic Kingdom across the water.
- **Alice in Wonderland splash area.** Themed water play area near the pool with shallow water features — designed for toddlers.
- **Walt Disney World Marathon / runDisney events.** The Grand Floridian sits along most race routes, so race weekends bring extra energy.

> **Pro Tip:** **Take the toddler to the Alice in Wonderland splash zone instead of the main pool** during peak hours. The water is shallower, it's designed for small kids, and you'll skip the chaos of older kids on the waterslide. Bring water shoes — the splash zone surface is grippy but textured.

## What Are the Dining Options at the Grand Floridian?

The Grand Floridian has some of the strongest dining of any Disney resort.

**Table Service:**
- **Victoria & Albert's** — AAA Five Diamond, prix-fixe, dress code, adults only (10+); the most upscale restaurant on Disney property
- **Cítricos** — signature dining, Mediterranean influences, smart casual
- **Narcoossee's** — waterfront dining with a view of the Magic Kingdom fireworks; signature steaks and seafood
- **1900 Park Fare** — character dining (breakfast and dinner; characters vary by meal)
- **Grand Floridian Cafe** — casual all-day dining, broad menu including kids' options

**Quick Service:**
- **Gasparilla Island Grill** — open all day, sandwiches, salads, kids' meals
- **Gerardo's Italian Kitchen at Disney's Grand Floridian** — quick-service Italian (limited hours)

**Lounges and Snacks:**
- **Enchanted Rose** — Beauty and the Beast-themed lounge with cocktails and small plates
- **Mizner's Lounge** — quieter, classic hotel bar
- **Garden View Tea Room** — afternoon tea, advance reservations required
- **Beach Pool Bar & Grill** — poolside

> **Pro Tip:** **Book Narcoossee's for dinner timed to Magic Kingdom fireworks.** Request a window or patio table when you make the reservation. The waterfront views over the Seven Seas Lagoon mean you watch the fireworks from your table — no jockeying for a spot on the beach, no fighting a toddler bedtime, no walking back through the crowds. Reservations open exactly **60 days before check-in at 6 AM Eastern** through the Disney app.

## What's the Pool Situation?

Two pools:

1. **Beach Pool (main)** — zero-entry, 181-foot waterslide themed as a water tower, Alice in Wonderland splash zone for toddlers, hot tub, pool bar
2. **Courtyard Pool (quiet)** — smaller, quieter, no waterslide

Both heated year-round. Towels provided. Lifeguards on duty during operating hours.

## What About Grandparents and Multigenerational Travel?

The Grand Floridian is arguably the **best Deluxe resort for multigenerational trips with grandparents.** Key points:

- **The vibe matches.** Grandparents who associate "vacation" with refined hotels feel at home here. The pipe organ, live piano, afternoon tea, and Victorian atmosphere all read as luxury rather than theme.
- **Walking path to Magic Kingdom** is gentle and shaded; grandparents with moderate mobility can manage it. Wheelchair-accessible.
- **Two- and three-bedroom Grand Villas** are some of the largest accommodations at Disney — designed for multigenerational stays.
- **Afternoon tea** in the Garden View Tea Room is a low-effort, high-impact shared experience that works across generations.
- **Victoria & Albert's** is a special-occasion dinner option for the adults while one parent stays back with the toddler.
- **Spa on-site.** Senses Spa offers spa services if grandma wants a few hours of pampering during a park day.

## What Did We Not Love?

Honest negatives:

- **The price.** This is the most expensive Disney Deluxe by a meaningful margin. The room you get for $1,000+ per night does not feel categorically better than a $700 room at the Polynesian.
- **Atmosphere skews adult.** The resort works for toddler families but doesn't feel built for them. The Polynesian has more "kid energy" baked in.
- **Outer buildings can be a hike.** If you don't get a room in or near Sugar Loaf, you're walking 7-10 minutes from your room to the monorail or the main lobby every time. With a stroller and a tired toddler, that matters.
- **Formal touches don't always land with a toddler.** Live piano in the lobby is lost on a 2-year-old who'd rather watch a geyser erupt.

We'd come back — but only on a special-occasion trip, ideally with grandparents joining.

## Grand Floridian vs. Polynesian — Which Magic Kingdom Resort Is Better for Toddlers?

Both are on the Magic Kingdom monorail loop. Key differences:

- **Polynesian** — high-energy theming, best beach, best toddler pool, best character breakfast at 'Ohana, lower price
- **Grand Floridian** — more formal Victorian atmosphere, walking path to Magic Kingdom, stronger dining lineup, higher price

For a toddler family on their first or second Disney trip, the Polynesian wins on toddler-fit and value. For a multigenerational trip with grandparents who want refined Disney, the Grand Floridian edges ahead.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is the Grand Floridian on the monorail?**
Yes. It's one of three resorts on the Magic Kingdom monorail loop, along with the Polynesian and the Contemporary.

**Can you walk to Magic Kingdom from the Grand Floridian?**
Yes. A paved, stroller-accessible walking path runs along the Seven Seas Lagoon from the Grand Floridian directly to Magic Kingdom. The walk takes 10-15 minutes.

**Is the Grand Floridian good for toddlers?**
Yes, but with a more formal atmosphere than the Polynesian or Animal Kingdom Lodge. The walking path to Magic Kingdom, zero-entry pool, and Alice in Wonderland splash zone are toddler highlights.

**How much does the Grand Floridian cost per night?**
Standard rooms run roughly $750-$1,200 per night. Theme Park View and Club Level run higher. Grand Villas are $4,000+.

**Does the Grand Floridian have free parking?**
Yes, self-parking is complimentary for all resort guests. Valet is available for an additional fee.

**Is Victoria & Albert's appropriate for kids?**
No. Victoria & Albert's has a strict 10-and-older minimum age requirement and a formal dress code. It is designed as an adults-only experience.

**Can you see Magic Kingdom fireworks from the Grand Floridian?**
Yes. The beach, lagoon-side restaurants (Narcoossee's), and lagoon-view rooms all have direct views of the fireworks. Piped-in music is available in select areas.

**Does the Grand Floridian have a kids' club?**
No. The on-site kids' club closed and has not reopened.

**What's afternoon tea at the Grand Floridian?**
A traditional afternoon tea service in the Garden View Tea Room with finger sandwiches, pastries, and a curated tea selection. Advance reservations required. A children's option is available.

**Are pets allowed at the Grand Floridian?**
No. The Grand Floridian is not one of Disney's pet-friendly resorts.

**Do you need a car at the Grand Floridian?**
No. Disney transportation reaches every park, Disney Springs, and the water parks. A car can be useful for off-property dining but is not required.

**What's the best room view at the Grand Floridian?**
Theme Park View rooms on upper floors offer a direct line of sight to Cinderella Castle. Lagoon View rooms have water views with partial castle views.

**Is the Grand Floridian quiet at night?**
The lobby has live music until late evening but quiets down by 10 PM. Outer buildings are quieter than the main building.

**Can you book the Grand Floridian without park tickets?**
Yes. Any Disney resort can be booked independently of park tickets.

**What's the best time to visit the Grand Floridian with a toddler?**
Early November and mid-January through mid-February typically offer lower crowds and milder Florida weather — ideal for toddler-paced touring.
